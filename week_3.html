Week 3 - Encoding Basics for Notation

Task 1 - Comparing MusicXML and MEI

The Files

  1. MusicXML
  2. MEI

Human Readability

  1. The primary focus of MusicXML is to be widely useable by many applications and to be easily transferable between them, as such, human readability is not great.
  2. MEI on the otherhand is designed with human readability in-mind. This, to me, is especially apparent in the way all information on a note is grouped on a single line, making it much easier to decern.

Hierarchy

  1. In MusicXML, the "note" tag is the parent of the "beam" tag, prioritising individual notes over groups.
  2. MEI takes the other approach of having a wider beam group containing child "note" tags containing further child information about octave, duration, stem etc.

Ornamentation

  1. MusicXML seems to not contain much information about ornamentation in the music. My piece features a few turns which MusicXML simply registers as a "turn" tag with no extra information.
  2. MEI however assigns ids to the turns and gives them their form as "upper".
